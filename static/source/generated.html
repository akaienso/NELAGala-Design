About the Event

Since 1989, the Sons of Italy Foundation’s (SIF) Annual National Education & Leadership Awards (NELA) Gala has raised millions of dollars and has hosted many distinguished individuals. Past attendees have included U.S. presidents, members of Congress and the Cabinet, and foreign diplomats. Previous SIF honorees include President Bill Clinton; President George W. Bush; Italy’s President Giorgio Napolitano; U.S. Supreme Court Justices Antonin Scalia and Samuel Alito; former Secretary of Defense Leon Panetta; former Fiat CEO Sergio Marchionne; science & medicine luminaries Dr. Anthony Fauci, Dr. Robert Gallo, and Dr. Michael Sofia; entertainers Frank Sinatra, Tony Bennett, Annette Funicello, and Gary Sinise; and sports legends Mario Andretti, Geno Auriemma, and Tommy Lasorda.

Founded in 1959, the SIF has contributed millions of dollars to scholarships, medical research, disaster relief efforts, cultural projects, veterans’ causes, and other special projects. The proceeds from the NELA Gala help fund its philanthropy.

Who's who...

NELA Gala Honorary Chair

Ambassador Mariangela Zappia

Mariangela Zappia, Italy's Ambassador to the United States, is a distinguished diplomat with over 35 years of experience. She's the first woman to hold several key diplomatic positions, including Permanent Representative to the UN in New York and to NATO. Zappia is an advocate for gender parity and has received recognition for her efforts. She holds a commendation from the Order of Merit of the Italian Republic and is fluent in English and French. She's authored works on UN reform and Italy's peacekeeping contributions and is a proud mother of two.

Master of Ceremonies

Joe Mantegna

Chicago-born Joe Mantegna boasts a diverse career spanning theater, film, and television. He earned acclaim for his portrayal of Richard Roma in David Mamet's "Glengarry Glen Ross," winning both a Tony and Joseph Jefferson Award. He co-created the Off-Broadway play "Bleacher Bums" inspired by his love for baseball. His film credits include roles in "The Godfather Part III," "Searching for Bobby Fischer," and "Criminal Minds," where he played FBI Agent David Rossi for thirteen seasons. This year marks his 19th consecutive year as host of the National Memorial Day Concert and the SIF NELA Gala.

Honorees

SIF National Education & Leadership Award

F. Anthony Naccarato

Anthony, a first-generation Italian American, has dedicated his life to his family and a remarkable career in the maritime industry spanning 58 years. He's held leadership positions at Ogden Marine Inc. and Crowley Maritime, receiving prestigious awards such as the Admiral of The Ocean Sea Award. Anthony is deeply involved in promoting maritime welfare and safety, serving on multiple boards and committees. His contributions have made him a distinguished leader in both industry and community.

SIF Excellence in Business Award

Jill M. Albertelli

Jill Albertelli is the President of Military Engines at Pratt & Whitney, overseeing the development and support of military offerings like the F-22 and F-35 engines. With over 30 years of industry experience, she holds a bachelor's degree in Engineering from Boston University and an MBA from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute. Jill is committed to advancing women in STEM fields and is actively involved in various organizations supporting this cause, such as the Society of Women Engineers and Women in Aviation International. She has been recognized for her achievements, including induction into the YMCA Academy of Women Achievers and receiving the National Diversity Council Influential Women award.

SIF Humanitarian Award

Augustin Tellez

Augustin "Augie" Tellez, has served as the executive vice president of the Seafarers International Union (SIU) since 2005, having previously led its collective bargaining from 1991. Starting with SIU in 1975 as an able-bodied seaman, Tellez climbed the ranks, significantly contributing to maritime training and safety through roles at the Paul Hall Center. His efforts have bolstered SIU members' benefits and played a pivotal role in maritime advisory councils and the U.S. military's sealift capabilities. Tellez, married to Susan Lagana since 1980, has three children and five grandchildren, embodying a life dedicated to maritime labor rights and family.

SIF Lifetime Achievement Award for Public Service

William “Bill” Pascrell, Jr.

Bill Pascrell, Jr., a New Jersey native, has served as Congressman for the 9th Congressional District since 1996. He's championed economic issues, healthcare, environmental protection, and tax fairness throughout his tenure. Pascrell has been a leading voice in restoring deductions and combating the opioid crisis. He's authored significant legislation supporting fire departments nationwide and has been a strong advocate for veterans, securing resources for VA clinics. As co-chair of the Italian-American Congressional Delegation, he promotes Italian-American heritage in Congress. Prior to his congressional career, Pascrell served in the U.S. Army and held various elected positions, including Mayor of Paterson. Recognized for his leadership and service, he's received numerous accolades from organizations. Pascrell and his wife, Elsie Marie Botto, have three children and five grandchildren.

SIF Lifetime Achievement Award in the Arts

Tony Lo Bianco

Tony Lo Bianco is a versatile actor with a career spanning over 68 years. He has starred in over 100 films, TV shows, and stage productions. He's known for his roles in "The French Connection," "The Honeymoon Killers," and "Bloodbrothers." He also received an Emmy Award for "Hizzoner! The Life of New York Mayor Fiorello La Guardia." As a director, his work includes "Police Story" and the feature film "Too Scared to Scream." He is actively involved in humanitarian efforts and is a proud supporter of veterans' causes.
